We are a group of modellers from various universities and research institutes who want to promote open energy modelling. We believe that Open Source models and Open Data will advance knowledge and lead to better energy policies. Our mission is to enable Open Source energy modelling by providing a platform for collaboration as well as tools along the full value chain of energy economics and energy system models. That is why we founded the Open Energy Modelling Initiative (openmod initiative). You are welcome to join!

Types of models & data

Below you can find a description of the types of data and models that we plan to collect an offer under permissive licenses.

  • Data
  • Data processing
  • Model

We aim to become a platform for linking to and sharing open, model-ready energy data. This includes, but is not limited to:

  • electricity demand
  • wind profiles
  • power plant portfolios
  • transmission network topology

Data we link to or provide will be shipped with a permissive Open Data license and in clearly specified and easy to process formats.

Processing data to make it machine readable is in an important and time consuming part of modelling, and efforts are frequently mirrored by several researchers. One example of this approach is a script to extract Cross-border Trading Data from the ENTSOE Transparency platform. The link can be found on the data page of our wiki.

On the wiki we provide structured information on our models. We aim to provide access to open source models for reasons of transparency, and also re-usability of code.
